Tourist Visa — Visa on Arrival (VOA)

Australian passports require a Visa on Arrival. No visa-free entry.

Cost$35 USD (~$50 AUD) per person — pay at airport counter or apply online
Duration30 days from arrival
Extension+30 days (another 500,000 IDR / ~$50 AUD) — apply at immigration office before day 30
RequirementsPassport with 6+ months validity, return flight booking
PaymentUSD cash, AUD, EUR, or credit card at the airport counter
Dom needs an extension! 31-day trip (Apr 9 - May 10) exceeds the 30-day VOA. Apply for extension around April 30 at any immigration office in Bali. Budget ~$50 AUD + a few hours at the office. Buddha (20 days, Apr 9-29) is fine without extending.
Skip the airport queue: Apply for e-VOA online via Molina (evisa.imigrasi.go.id) up to 14 days before arrival. Pay online, get a QR code, skip the payment counter at DPS. The immigration queue alone can be 30-60 min without it.
No QLD motorcycle licence — Use Grab/Gojek only for transport. No scooter rentals.

  • Midnight pickup from hotel
  • Drive to Paltuding trailhead (~1 hr)
  • Trek to crater (3km, 1.5-2 hrs up)
  • BLUE FLAMES visible until ~4:30 AM
  • Sunrise over turquoise crater lake
  • Descend, return to hotel, rest
  • Afternoon: explore Banyuwangi or visit Jagir Waterfall
Trek tips: Gas mask required (~$4.50 AUD rental). Bring warm layers (2,386m elevation), sturdy shoes, headlamp. Entrance ~$10-15 AUD.

Day 4: April 12 (Sun) — Bromo or Surabaya

Option A: Direct to Surabaya (train/bus/shuttle)

Day 5: April 13 (Mon) — Bromo Sunrise or Surabaya

  • If Bromo: 3am jeep to King Kong Hill viewpoint, sunrise over crater, climb 253 steps, then drive to Surabaya (~3-4 hrs)
  • If Surabaya: Explore — House of Sampoerna, Submarine Monument, Arab Quarter

Days 6-7: April 14-15 — Surabaya & Madura Island

  • Madura Island day trip — Jimmy Malay's tombstone says he's from Madura. Cross the Suramadu Bridge (~5.4 km, longest in Indonesia). Visit Bangkalan (closest town) or Pamekasan for archives/churches. Grab won't work on Madura — arrange return driver
  • City exploration: House of Sampoerna, Arab Quarter & Ampel Mosque, local markets
  • Family history archives at Jl. Jagir Wonokromo No. 350 (colonial records research)
Madura tip: The island is connected to Surabaya by the Suramadu Bridge — drive across, no ferry needed. ~30 min to Bangkalan. Madura has its own Malay-influenced culture, old mosques, and Dutch-era records. If Jimmy Malay's tombstone says Madura, this is the primary lead. Arrange a private driver for the day (~$105-125 AUD for 12 hrs) through your hotel — ride-hailing is unreliable on Madura.

Madura Island — Heritage Day Trip

Jimmy Malay's tombstone says he's from Madura. "Malay" was a colonial catch-all term — in Australia, it covered all Southeast Asians (Javanese, Madurese, Bugis, etc.). If the tombstone says Madura, he was likely Madurese — a distinct ethnic group of maritime traders and seafarers, many of whom came to Australia as pearl divers and indentured labourers from the 1870s onward.

Transport: Private car + English-speaking driver for 12 hrs: ~$105-125 AUD all-in (fuel, bridge toll-free, pickup/dropoff). Book via Wandernesia or through your hotel. Grab doesn't work reliably beyond Bangkalan.
Practical: Madura is 95% Muslim — dress modestly (long pants, covered shoulders). No alcohol. Carry cash (IDR). Almost no English spoken — the driver is your translator. Bring a printed photo of Jimmy Malay's tombstone to show museum staff and locals. Download Google Translate offline Indonesian before going.
Pre-trip homework: Search FamilySearch for "Madoera" (Dutch spelling) civil registration records (1806-1997). Check the Indische Genealogical Society (IGV) — 2M+ Dutch East Indies records. Contact Museum Keraton Sumenep in advance to explain your research.

This section is Dom-only. Buddha, you're on a plane home by now. But have a read if you're curious about the family history adventure!

Tracing the roots of James Peter Subtino Solien ("Jimmy Malay") — Dom's ancestor, a maritime trader. His tombstone says he's from Madura Island (visited from Surabaya in Leg 1).
